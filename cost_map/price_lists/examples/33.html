* What's included?

  • The prices in the table above (the "Guide Prices") show what most patients should expect to pay to undergo the procedure listed above (the "Procedure").
  • The Guide Prices for your procedure might be different depending on:
    • Which consultant or specialist you choose
    • Your medical history
    • The type of anaesthetic your consultant advises is best for you
    • The type of implant you choose or your consultant advises is best for you
  • Your price quotation will be made clear to you before you go ahead with any tests, consultations or proceed for treatment (see the "Your quote" section below).
  • The Guide Prices are correct as at May 2013.

Click here for more information

  • The "Initial Consultation" Guide Price covers your initial appointment with your consultant (surgeon).
  • The "Diagnostic Investigations" Guide Price covers the cost of diagnostic tests (such as x-rays and blood tests) that most patients need when diagnosing your condition. Your consultant might advise that you need additional diagnostic investigations. If this is the case, the hospital will tell you the prices before you go ahead with them.
  • The "Main Treatment" Guide Price covers your stay in hospital, as long as your consultant advises that it is necessary (but not exceeding 30 days). It includes all the tests you need, fees for your consultant and anaesthetist, nursing care, medication and food during this time.
  • The "Post Discharge Care" Guide Price includes your care after you leave hospital, including up to one follow-up appointment (unless otherwise stated), removal of stitches, change of dressings and the medication you need to take home with you for up to 14 days after you leave hospital. Please note that some home aids (such as commodes) are not included in the Guide Price.
  • The Guide Price table shows any additional care included in the Main Treatment Price, for example, up to 6 physiotherapy sessions (if appropriate).
  • The "Main Treatment" Guide Price includes prosthesis (for example, hip or knee implants) from a standard range most commonly used for the procedure. If your consultant advises that you need different prosthesis, then the price for your procedure may be different. Your final price quote will be made clear to you before you proceed to treatment (see the "Your quote" section below).

Your quote

  • Before going ahead with a procedure, you will need to have an initial consultation with a consultant to talk about your medical history and what treatment you need as part of the Procedure. You will need to pay for this consultation, as well as any associated diagnostic tests (for example x-rays and blood tests), even if you decide not to go ahead with the procedure.
  • After your initial consultation, the hospital will provide you with a final price quote for your procedure
